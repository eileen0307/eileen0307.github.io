About Me

Where I Come From Shapes How I See

My story begins in El Salvador, a small but vibrant country in Central America. At nine years old, my family and I made the journey to Texas, and it was through that experience of leaving everything familiar behind that I truly learned the value of geography. A place does not just shape your point of view — it shapes who you are.

That curiosity for people, places, and environments has been the thread running through my life and is what led me to where I am today. That passion brought me to Iowa State University, where I studied Environmental Science with a minor in Geographic Information Systems.

Through my coursework, internships, and professional work, I developed hands-on experience in spatial analysis, cartographic design, and data-driven storytelling. I work primarily with the ESRI suite of products including Field Maps, Experience Builder, and Dashboards, as well as Python, ArcGIS Online, and Mapbox.

Featured Tool

Percent Hydric Soils Calculator

As a GIS consultant working extensively with environmental data, one of my most recurring tasks involved calculating the percentage of hydric soils within a given study area. Hydric soils are a key indicator of wetland presence, making their accurate measurement critical in environmental assessments.

The process was incredibly tedious — requiring manual data retrieval from the USDA NRCS Web Soil Survey, boundary creation, area calculations, unit conversions, and percentage analysis across multiple sites. That frustration became the motivation behind my very first custom GIS tool, which automated this workflow and transformed a laborious multi-step process into something fast, repeatable, and reliable.
